						<div class="advPubTkt-content"><p>
  <em><strong>Please Note that this Section is a General Season Overview written in August 2014.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <a href="http://www.sawtoothunited.com/home.php?layout=314696"><span style="color: #ff0000;"><em><strong>For daily updated FALL 2014 TEAM UPDATES, CLICK HERE.</strong></em></span></a>
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>Greetings Sawtooth United Players, Parents and Coaches! After an amazing summer of the 2014 World Cup in Brazil and 2 incredible weeks of ONE Soccer camps right here in the Wood River
  Valley, we are now ready for an exciting Fall 2014 Soccer Season!</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>Here is a quick overview of the SUFC Fall 2014 Season:</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  -<em><strong>SUFC is offering 6 teams:</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>1) <strong>Boys U10--STUMPH--Coached by Trent Stumph.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>2) Boys U10--HOLTER--Coached by Dean Holter, Michael Desler, and Ted Kelley.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>3) <strong>Boys U11--Coached by Eric Slotten and Clay Sammis.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>4) <strong>Boys U12--Coached by Marco Hidalgo and Chris Gardner.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>5) <strong>Girls U10/U11--Coached by Sally Gerlach and Clint Kelly.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  <em>6) <strong>Girls U12--Coached by Dave Hattula and Toni Hattula.</strong></em>
</p>
<p>
  -<em><strong>Season Begin/E</strong><strong>nd</strong><strong>:</strong> The season will officially <strong>begin</strong> on Tuesday, September 2, 2014. That will be the first week of official
  practices; days and times to be determined by each coach (but likely Tues/Thurs, 5:30-7pm practice times).</em> Coaches may start practicing in August at their discretion. Your team's coach may
  start contacting you in August with August informal practice schedule information. The season will <strong>end</strong> the week of Halloween, October 31st.
</p>
<p>
  -<em><strong>Player Registration Fee is $260:</strong></em>This fee to include twice weekly practices and the following games schedule:
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>1) Boise League, Two Saturdays, September 20th and October 18th</strong></em> (two games each Saturday): This is the Idaho Youth Soccer Association ("IYSA") Boise D3 League at the Boise
  Simplot Soccer Complex. Competition is strong and our teams often lose. We have researched the less competitive IYSA "Outback" Recreation League as an alternative to the more hyper-competitive
  Boise D3 League. However, the Outback games are played as far away as McCall with lesser quality fields, ref-ing, etc. After much research and discussion SUFC has decided to stick with the Boise D3
  League and focus on raising our game to be able to compete at this higher level.
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>2)</strong> <strong>IYSA Gem State Tournament October 4-5th,</strong></em> also at the Boise Simplot Soccer Complex. This is also a full "competitive" tournament, with 2-3 games on
  Saturday and 1-2 games on Sunday. It is a full weekend of competitive tournament soccer. SUFC's goal is to be increasingly more competitive at this high level of competition.
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>3)</strong> <strong>"Friendlies" to be scheduled against some/all of the following:</strong></em> - Local BCRD Rec team/s, - Local La Liga team/s, - Mt. Home club team/s, - Twin Falls
  club team/s.
</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">
  <em><strong>-Why?</strong></em> SUFC has gotten lots of feedback from parents who want to stay and play local whenever possible. We are listening. While it is important to go play the competitive
  Boise D3 League, we are also working this fall to "play local", with games yet to be determined as we make the connections and fill out this fall season's dance card.
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>4) Two Optional Tournaments:</strong></em>
</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">
  1- Jackson Hole Tournament, September 26-28th; registration deadline is September 1, 2014.
</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">
  2- FC Nova Fall Star Showcase Tournament, Boise, November 1-2nd; registration deadline is October 10, 2014.
</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">
  *** These two tournaments are NOT part of the SUFC regular season. However, a coach/team can rally to play one of these very fun and quality tournaments at a cost of about $50-75 per player.
  Contact your coach for further details if interested.
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>-Fall Season vs. Spring Season Background:</strong> SUFC offers 2 full soccer programs each <strong>Spring</strong></em>; which is the Developmental Program, ages U6-U9; and the
  Competitive Program, ages U10-U17.
</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">
  <em><strong>-However, SUFC offers only a limited Competitive Program in the Fall</strong></em> so as to not compete for players at the Developmental Program level with the local Recreation Programs
  offered by Atkinson Rec in Ketchum, and BCRD in Hailey; and so as not to compete for players at the Competitive Program level with the WR Valley's Middle and High School teams. Sawtooth is also
  increasingly working closely with the Atkinson Rec and BCRD programs synergistically to improve the effectiveness of all soccer in the WR Valley. For example, this fall, Sawtooth will offer tips
  and tricks to A-REC and BCRD Rec coaches in a fall coaching clinic.
</p>
<p>
  <em><strong>-Wednesday Night "<em><strong>SAWTOOTH COMP CLINIC</strong></em>": This fall, SUFC will offer a one-day-per-week competitive training clinic, aka the "SAWTOOTH COMP CLINIC" (likely
  Wednesdays, check back after Labor Day for details) for ages U6-U9, and U10-U12. This Wednesday evening clinic will offer an extra night of specialized soccer training for those players who want
  additional "touches on the ball" each week. We offered this two falls ago with great positive feedback. (This has been changed to the "SKILLS CLINIC", see top of page).</strong></em>
</p></div>					</div>

  through the sport of soccer.

  possible to carry out such outstanding developmental and competitive youth soccer programs," stated Liv Jensen, SUFC Coach and Board Member. "Thank you, Redfish."
