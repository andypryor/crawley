<p><span class="style1"><br />
  BNSC Coaches Code of Conduct</span><br />
  <br />
  <strong>As a BNSC coach, you have the following responsibilities:<br />
</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Be a good role model. Demonstrate self-control, integrity, and professional behavior on and off the field.</li>
  <li>Show respect for players, parents, opponents and referees.</li>
  <li>Demonstrate good sportsmanship. Maintain a balanced perspective.</li>
  <li>Support the principles of BNSC and follow the direction/curriculum established by the DOC.</li>
  <li>Run organized and challenging team practices.</li>
  <li>Attend and be on time for practices and games.</li>
  <li>Ensure that the travel plans and expectations of the team are clearly communicated early in the season.</li>
  <li>Clearly communicate team goals and expectations to parents and players.</li>
  <li>Provide players and parents with written player evaluations each season.</li>
  <li>Be approachable to players and parents to help them resolve problems.</li>
  <li>Be dedicated to player development. Praise effort, not just good results. Teach and inspire.</li>
  <li>Challenge individual players to improve their skills and understanding of the game.</li>
  <li>Use positive reinforcement and use appropriate and consistent discipline for inappropriate behavior.</li>
  <li>Ensure that the players have an emotionally and physically safe atmosphere to learn and play soccer.</li>
  <li>Recommend that an injured player receive proper medical treatment.</li>
  <li>Ensure that a medical release for each player, a first aid kit and ice are available at every practice and game.</li>
  <li>Reinforce and support the BNSC tradition of academic excellence and community involvement.</li>
  <li>Cooperate with players and their other extra-curricular events.</li>
  <li>Work with your team manager, parents and DOC to ensure the best team harmony and to create a fun environment. In addition, support and foster club level activities.</li>
  <li>Continue upgrading your soccer education and proficiency as a coach.</li>
  <li>Maintain equipment that is issued to you and return it to BNSC at the end of the playing year.</li>
  <li>Adhere to the BNSC Code of Conduct and the BNSC Handbook.<br />
    <br />
    <br />
  </li>
</ul>
